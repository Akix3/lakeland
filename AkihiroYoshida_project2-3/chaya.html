Chaya District

Higashi Chaya District

    Higashi Chaya district was the biggest and most flourishing chaya district in Kanazawa. The area of this district is about 1.8ha. There are a lot of traditional Chaya style buildings which were bulit in the Edo era and the early Meiji era, so the district is designated as Important Preservation Districts for Groups of Traditional Buildings by Japanese government. There are many restaurants, teahouses, and souvenir shops. Most popular and photogenic souvenir is gold leaf. Kanazawa account for 99 percent of all production of gold leaf in Japan. By the way, the Kanji of “Kanazawa” means “a lot of gold”. Please enjoy golden city, Kanazawa!

Kazuemachi Chaya District

    Kazuemachi chaya district is located aside of Asanogawa river. There are many restaurants and teahouses, and this area has a great ambience. In spring, you can see the combination of full of cherry blossom trees, Asanogawa river, and traditional buildings. There are many photogenic spots known fir those in the know.
