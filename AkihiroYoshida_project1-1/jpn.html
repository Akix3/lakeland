JAPANESE TRADITIONAL GARDEN

HISTORY

The first style of Shinjyuku Gyoen garden was only Japanese traditional style. In the Edo era, this site was owned by a “daimyo” (feudal lord, Naito clan). He constructed this garden centered around Tamamo Pond. Up to the present time, there are many difficulties, but this garden has been preserved by a lot of efforts and made better and better.

HIGHLIGHTS

Tamamo Pond

Tamamo Pond is the first garden made in this site, so it can be called the origin of Shinjyuku Gyoen. There are beautiful Japanese style pond, bridge, stone-lantern, and plants. Sometimes you can see cute fishes and birds in the pond! In addition, behind this garden, there are many skyscrappers. The collaboration of traditional Japanese Garden and morderm bulidings is very cool.

Flowers

There are many Japanese plants. You can see noble pine trees at all seasons. However, the best season is spring. In early spring, you can see plum blossoms (Ume, in Japnese), and in late spring, you can see cherry blossomes (Sakura, in Japanese). The soft pastel pink is one of the colors which represent Japan.
